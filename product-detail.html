VIBRO COMPACTION

Vibro Compaction is a technique that enables the rearrangement of soil particles into a denser configuration with vibration by the use of powerful downhole deep vibrating probes.

Vibro Replacement (Stone Columns) uses the same principle of the Vibro Compaction (powerful vibro flots that generate vibration to re-arrange soil particles) but in soils where the VC is not possible due to higher fines content (higher than 10% and - typically less than 40%); thus, the densification is achieved by displacement of the soils radially by the vibrator, refilling the resulting space with granular material which results in the construction of stone columns at the treatment locations. While Vibro Compaction is more of a Densification Ground Improvement technique that rearranges the soil particles into a denser state, Vibro Replacement (Stone Columns) is a Reinforcement GI technique that uses the same principle and equipment than the VC, but focuses into the strengthening of the soil by the construction of a stone column that enhances the soil bearing capacity, increases the shear strength and creates drainage paths for the excess water pressure.

Applicable Soils & Depth of Improvement

The Vibro Compaction technique is most suitable for loose medium to coarse-grained sands with silt / clay content less than 10% (less efficient with fines content between 10% and 15%). Can be applicable to depths up to 45m (or more depending on Crane capacity) The Stone Columns are most suited for sands with fines content greater than 10% and typically less than 40% or for clays which require some level of bearing or lateral reinforcement. Can be applicable to depths up to 45m (or more depending on Crane capacity)

Applications

  • Nearly all reclamation projects
  • buildings
  • offshore and nearshore man-made islands
  • pipelines
  • road and rail embankments
  • power plants
  • infrastructure in general

Advantages

  • Increases the allowable bearing capacity of the soil
  • Limits (minimizes) total and differential settlements
  • Mitigates the risk of Liquefaction (to the design depth) in case of seismic event
  • Reduces the size of foundation due to reinforced ground

Disadvantages

  • Vibro Compaction has a diminished effectiveness if sand has fines content between 10% to 15% and definitively is not applicable if fines content in excess of 15%, unless combined with other G.I. approaches.
